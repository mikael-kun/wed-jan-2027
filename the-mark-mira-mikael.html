Sun & Moon · 日と月

Her Sun sits on your Moon — the oldest sentence two charts can say to each other: the two lights become one. That conjunction is the natural seed of a marriage mark, and it carries a tradition older than astrology apps: the alchemical coniunctio, the wedding of Sol and Luna.

IISeal · 印

Sigillum

The same conjunction sealed inside a ring — a wax-stamp, a coin, a closing mark. The bolt threads between the two lights: Sol and Luna struck together. Built for the order-of-service cover, foil, or a literal seal.

IVOne horizon · 地平

Horizon

Your daylight-bar motif, distilled. Her Sun rising, your Moon setting on a single line of sky, the spark where they cross. The quietest of the four, and the one that already rhymes with the site.

Two notes on the craft

On the glyphs. The raw ☉ ☽ ♅ characters stay out of these on purpose — they read as chart-notation, and ♅ turns to a tofu box on half of phones. Uranus is honoured by its meaning instead: the spark, the strike. The astrology stays underneath; the surface stays a mark.

A system, not one logo. My read: I (Coniunctio) as the everyday mark — wordmark lockup, favicon, the site header; II (Sigillum) as the ceremonial seal for the order-of-service cover and any foil or wax. III and IV are alternates if you want the union or the horizon read to lead.
